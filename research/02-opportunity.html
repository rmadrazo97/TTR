The Recovery Opportunity

Three distinct tax pools, one shared ingestion pipeline. Together they total ~€6,000 per international truck per year — before driver income-tax relief.

2.1 Three stackable revenue streams

2.2 Stream A — Cross-border VAT refund (Directive 2008/9/EC, successor to the 8th Directive)

A carrier VAT-registered at home but not in the country where it bought diesel/tolls/repairs reclaims that foreign VAT electronically through its home portal — it does not file a foreign return.2

  • Deadline: 30 September of the following year — hard, no extensions.1
  • Minimum claim size (Art. 17): you can't file below €400 for a part-year claim (3–11 months), or €50 for a full year.1
  • Invoice copies: the portal wants an image of the actual invoice once an item's pre-tax value tops €1,000 — but only €250 for fuel. Most diesel fills clear €250, so nearly every fuel claim needs the full invoice → our ingestion must capture invoices, not just card slips.
  • Timeline: the refund country decides in 4 months (6–8 if it queries); interest is owed on delay.
  • Non-EU carriers (UK, CH, TR) use the slower, paper-based 13th Directive.2

Legal wrinkle — the fuel-card question (CJEU Vega International C-235/18)

Diesel bought on a fuel card can count as the issuer's VAT-exempt credit, not a fuel supply — so the pump receipt may not be the valid invoice. Our pipeline must know whose invoice it holds (station vs issuer). Stream A's biggest legal risk.

2.3 Stream B — Professional-diesel excise refunds

  • What it is: a partial refund of national diesel excise, down to a lower "commercial diesel" floor.4
  • Who qualifies: trucks with gross vehicle weight ≥ 7.5 t (universal across the schemes).
  • Watch the rates: volatile (quarterly/annual) and vary sharply by country — the platform must treat them as live data.

Shrinking pool: Italy gates to Euro 5+, France/Belgium rates are on a legislated downward path, and the EU Energy Taxation Directive recast may end commercial-diesel carve-outs.

2.4 Stream C — Per-diem & days-abroad income tax (driver-level)

Tax-free daily allowances for meals and in-cab overnights cut the driver's taxable income — and, in Poland/Romania/Lithuania, the social-contribution base (a much bigger lever). It's a count-the-days problem, and the proof already lives in tachograph/GPS data. For execution: treat Stream C as a driver-level upsell, not the beachhead's core — its value hinges on whether the driver is employed or a self-employed autónomo (the two cases below).

Worked example — Germany (employed driver)

A driver with ~220 travel days recovers ~€7,680 in meal + overnight allowances vs the €1,230 default lump sum5 → ~€1,600–1,900 cash refund per year, recurring. In PL/RO/LT the contribution-base reduction can be worth several thousand € more.

Employed drivers (payroll) — where Stream C is a product

The exemptions above apply to employed drivers. For a carrier that employs drivers, structuring tax-free meal + overnight allowances is a real, recurring lever (DE example: ~€1,600–1,900 / driver / yr; larger via the contribution base in PL/RO/LT). Here Stream C is a payroll-structuring product — sold to the employer.

Self-employed autónomo — our beachhead (small lever)

A self-employed autónomo can't pay himself an exempt dieta — he deducts only limited meal costs (ES: €26.67 domestic / €48.08 abroad, requiring electronic payment at a hospitality venue; in-cab meals don't qualify). So for the autónomo-first pilot, Stream C is a modest deduction, not a big refund. Don't size the beachhead on it.

2.5 Why the money goes unclaimed — our wedge

  • Invoice-format rules: a card slip or simplified till receipt is rejected — needs full supplier name, address, VAT ID.
  • Multi-portal friction: every country is a different form, software, and language.
  • Cash-flow lag: VAT 4–8 months; some excise 6–18 months.
  • Thresholds & deadlines: below €400/€50 discourages the small operator; miss 30 Sep and it's gone.
  • Record-keeping: per-diem relief needs proof of days/overnights — data drivers rarely reconstruct.
  • Under-claiming is systemic — the entire specialist-agent industry exists on this gap.
Look-back — the first-engagement multiplier

A new customer can usually reclaim several prior years at once, not just the current one. Each stream has its own look-back window:

  • Spanish IRPF (rectificación) — 4 years back
  • German income tax — 4 years back
  • French excise — ~2 years back

→ First-year recovery is often 2–4× the normal annual run-rate. That makes look-back a strong market-opening hook — and a de-risker for the pilot's per-truck recovery target.
