Risks & Recommendations

The opportunity is real and the lane is open. The risks are concentrated in two soft numbers and a handful of regulatory realities — all testable in 90 days.

7.2 Regulatory constraints to design around

  • Hard 30 Sep VAT deadline — miss it and the year's VAT is lost. "We handle the deadline" is both a feature and an obligation.
  • Invoice-validity rules — simplified/card-slip receipts are rejected; the platform must flag and fix at ingestion.
  • Excise rates are volatile (quarterly/annual) and country-specific — must be live config, never hardcoded.
  • Per-diem eligibility gates — salary thresholds (PL, LT), 3× cap (RO), nights-in-cab proof (DE) — high compliance surface.
  • Mobility Package — subsistence allowances don't count toward host minimum wage; A1 form preserves home-country optimization.
  • Licensed filing in several states — structure as tech + licensed-agent partnership, not unlicensed advice.

7.4 Open questions to resolve before scaling

  1. What is the real success-fee owner-operators will accept — and is our blended 15% defensible against agents at 5–12% who also pre-finance?
  2. What share of the ~6.2M EU trucks are genuinely international-active (the TAM's load-bearing input)?
  3. Will a fuel-card major expose transaction data via API/partnership — or must we stay OCR-only?
  4. What is the licensing / liability structure per country for filing on a customer's behalf?
  5. Does the gasóleo enrolment hook convert Spanish owner-operators as cheaply as the community channels imply?

Final recommendation

Proceed to an 8-week concierge pilot in Spain. The market is large (~€1.6B fee TAM), the segment is genuinely underserved, the timing (national e-invoicing mandates) is favourable, and the whole thesis rests on two numbers a cheap pilot can settle. Anchor on foreign VAT + excise, use gasóleo enrolment as the trust hook, distribute through trucker communities + Fenadismer, and price no-win-no-fee. Build nothing durable until € recovery is identified and claims are filed for real drivers (cash lands 4–8 months later — the pilot proves the funnel, not the payout).
