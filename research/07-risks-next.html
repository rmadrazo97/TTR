Risks & Recommendations

The opportunity is real and the lane is open. The risks are concentrated in two soft numbers and a handful of regulatory realities — all testable in 90 days.

7.1 Key risks & mitigations

High4 risks — the ones that could kill the business
Unit economics soft — €/truck, fee %, CAC & cost-to-serve are estimates.→ pilot measures € identified/filed, cost-to-serve & WTP before any durable build
Pre-financing = working capital — fronting refunds the state repays in 4–18 mo is a lending business.→ partner with refund-factoring (Eurowag/Factris model); defer until after POC
Clawback & representative liability — rejected/audited claim after the fee's booked; filer jointly liable.→ fee only on cash received · clawback reserve · E&O · conservative validity gating · per-country liability map
Fraud magnet — bulk upload invites duplicate/forged/over-litre claims; AEAT systemic-bad-filer flag.→ KYB · cross-customer dedup · anomaly detection (litres>tank) · human sign-off · fraud-loss provision
Medium7 risks — manageable by design choices
Eligibility floors — ≥7.5t excise, VAT registration, €50–400 minimums exclude the smallest.→ validate per country; target ≥7.5t international first
Data access — fuel-card majors control clean transaction data.→ OCR removes dependence; pursue a card API/partnership in parallel
Extraction accuracy — a wrong VAT ID kills a claim.→ dual-extractor reconcile + confidence gating + mandatory human sign-off
Per-diem legislated down (Mobility Package).→ anchor on fuel VAT + excise; per-diem is upsell, not core
Licensing by function/country — DE StBerG reserves filing; PSD2/AML if funds route through us.→ ES apoderamiento/colaborador social; never touch client money early; licence map per country
Fuel-card VAT recharacterisation (CJEU Vega Int'l) — card slip may not be a valid invoice.→ capture issuer invoice; know whose supply before filing (§2.2)
Regulated advice / gestor loyalty.→ partner licensed asesores; own the customer, white-label filing; gestor rev-share
Low–Med2 risks — monitor, don't over-invest
Incumbent response — FastVAT/Eurowag/ES tax-software add self-serve.→ move fast: community distribution + apoderamiento lock-in + orphaned excise/dietas bundle (§4.5)
GDPR / data privacy — receipts & tacho data are personal data.→ EU hosting, encryption, DPA, retention/deletion, audit log from day one

7.2 Regulatory constraints to design around

  • Hard 30 Sep VAT deadline — miss it, the year's VAT is lost; a feature and an obligation.
  • Invoice-validity rules — card slips rejected; flag & fix at ingestion.
  • Excise rates volatile & country-specific — live config, never hardcoded.
  • Per-diem gates — salary thresholds (PL, LT) · 3× cap (RO) · nights-in-cab proof (DE).
  • Mobility Package — allowances don't count toward host minimum wage; A1 form preserves home-country optimisation.
  • Licensed filing in several states — structure as tech + licensed-agent partnership.

7.3 The 90-day plan

  1. Days 0–30 · Validate demand Recruit 25–40 Spanish international carriers via Fenadismer + FB · stand up WhatsApp + email ingest + a simple admin console · contract one licensed asesor (filing + apoderamiento flow) · measure: do they grant filing rights & send docs?
  2. Days 30–60 · Validate € Run concierge claims end-to-end — file modelo 360 + gasóleo enrolment · confirm € identified & filed / truck + extraction accuracy · test WTP for a ~15% success fee · (cash lands month 4–8 — track filed, not paid)
  3. Days 60–90 · Decide & build Go/no-go on the two load-bearing numbers (€/truck filed · fee %) · if go, start MVP (PWA + foreign-VAT flow) · open a fuel-card partnership conversation · raise on POC evidence, not slides

7.4 Open questions to resolve before scaling

  1. Real success-fee owner-operators accept — is blended 15% defensible vs agents at 5–12% who also pre-finance?
  2. What share of ~6.2M EU trucks is genuinely international-active (the TAM's load-bearing input)?
  3. Will a fuel-card major expose transaction data — or are we OCR-only?
  4. Licensing / liability structure per country for filing on a customer's behalf?
  5. Does the gasóleo enrolment hook convert Spanish owner-operators as cheaply as community channels imply?

7.5 The deal — team, ask & milestones

Founders to complete before the raise

Team / why-us: articulate the founders' unfair advantage (transport/tax domain, Spain distribution, or AI/document-extraction depth) and why this team closes the lane before Eurowag. Ask & use of funds: size the pre-seed to reach the milestones below — team, one licensed asesor partner, ~12–18 months runway. Placeholders — fill with real numbers.

Wedge → platform vision: tax recovery is the wedge; the data asset (fuel, routes, filed claims, apoderamientos) opens embedded finance for carriers — factoring/pre-financing, fuel & toll optimisation, insurance. Comparables/acquirers: Taxfix (consumer tax UX), Eurowag (listed mobility platform), VAT IT / Blue Dot (VAT-tech M&A) — the fuel-card majors are the obvious strategic buyers.

Final recommendation

Proceed to an 8-week concierge pilot in Spain.

  • Why: large market (~€1.6B fee TAM), a genuinely underserved segment, favourable timing (e-invoicing mandates) — and the whole thesis rests on two numbers a cheap pilot can settle.
  • How: anchor on foreign VAT + excise · gasóleo enrolment as the trust hook · distribute via trucker communities + Fenadismer · price no-win-no-fee.
  • Discipline: build nothing durable until € is identified and claims are filed for real drivers (cash lands 4–8 months later).
