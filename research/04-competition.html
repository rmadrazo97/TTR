Competitive Landscape

Three overlapping layers of incumbents — all built for fleets, none for the owner-operator. The white space is a card-agnostic, self-serve, micro-priced bundle.

4.1 Three layers of incumbents

1 · Fuel-card majors

DKV, UTA Edenred, Eurowag, AS24, Shell, Circle K. Recovery is a retention feature on their own card spend + pre-financing. Card lock-in; third-party fuel falls out of scope.

2 · Reclaim specialists

FastVAT, EUROVAT, Vialtis, Négométal, Move Expert, REMOBIS. Card-agnostic but consultative, success-fee, "email us your invoices." Claim minimums filter out the smallest operators.

3 · Enterprise VAT-tech

VAT4U, Fintua (ex-Taxback), VAT IT, Blue Dot. Genuinely AI-driven but sell to multinational AP/finance teams — not a driver with one truck; weak on HGV excise.

4.3 Positioning — the open lane

4.4 Our wedge

The uncontested position

No player is simultaneously (a) card-agnostic, (b) self-serve/app-native, (c) micro-fleet-priced, and (d) bundling all three recoveries — fuel VAT + professional-diesel excise + income-tax per-diems. Per-diem handling in particular is orphaned between German "Spesen" tooling and generic expense apps, never alongside cross-border fuel recovery. That trifecta bundle for owner-operators is the open lane. Live search found no funded startup targeting self-serve tax recovery for micro-carriers (absence-of-evidence, not proof).

Risks to the wedge — de-risk early

Eligibility floors (≥7.5t for excise; VAT registration; €50–400 minimums) may exclude the very smallest owner-ops — validate per country. Data access: the fuel-card majors control clean transaction data that strengthens our product, but the OCR path keeps us independent of it — pursue an API/partnership in parallel, not as a dependency. Net-invoicing: as cards net VAT at source, the reclaimable pool on card spend shrinks — a structural reason to prioritise mixed/independent fuelling.

4.5 Emerging threats & our moat

Who could copy us

  • Eurowag / FastVAT ship a self-serve app — most likely response. Rated below "Low-Med": they have channel + licences, but cannibalising a card/consulting margin and re-tooling for micro self-serve is a real innovator's-dilemma drag.
  • TMS / telematics (Dashdoc — already sells TICPE recovery; Webfleet, Trimble, tachograph players) bolt tax onto the day/route data they own.
  • ES autónomo tax-software (Declarando, TaxDown, Holded) add modelo 360 to an existing gestor-replacement relationship.
  • Regional player Andamur (Murcia) already fuses card + recovery in our beachhead.

Why we get harder to copy

  • Apoderamiento stickiness: once a carrier grants us filing rights + connects their certificate, switching is friction — a genuine lock-in incumbents don't have with a card.
  • Filed-claim history & per-country rules data compound: every rejection sharpens the rules engine and validity checks — accuracy widens with volume.
  • Bundle breadth: VAT + excise + dietas in one relationship raises LTV and referral value the single-product incumbents can't match.
  • Community distribution (Fenadismer + FB) is a low-CAC channel that rewards the first credible entrant.
Honest read

The moat is execution + accumulation, not a patent. The defensible sequence is: win community trust → accumulate apoderamientos and claim-history data → widen coverage per customer faster than a card major will cannibalise its own margin. If we're slow, Eurowag or an ES tax-software incumbent closes the lane.
