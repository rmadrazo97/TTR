The correction that shapes the pilot

Domestic gasóleo profesional is already semi-automated — the fuel-card issuer reports to AEAT weekly and the refund is paid monthly, automatically. It is a great onboarding hook and trust signal ("we make sure you're actually enrolled and capturing your €2,450/truck"), but it is not where the money is. The wedge is the manual, slow, painful foreign VAT + excise on international runs — plus dietas IRPF optimization. Sell the assurance; monetise the recovery.

8.1 Why Spain — market snapshot

  • Atomised: 53.8% of freight firms own a single vehicle; 81.3% own ≤5. Our ICP is the whole market.
  • ~360,000 trucks; 287,489 public-service authorisations (+3.7% YoY).
  • Aging, non-digital-native buyer: avg driver age >50, half over 55 — trust & simplicity beat slick UX.
  • Driver shortage 20–30k+ — cost discipline matters; recovered € is real money.

Where to aim first

Murcia, the Mediterranean corridor (Valencia/Cataluña) and Aragón over-index on long-haul international runs — the exact profile where foreign-VAT recovery pays. A driver who runs FR/DE/PT weekly leaves the most on the table.

Tailwind

Verifactu (RD 1007/2023) and Crea-y-Crece B2B e-invoicing phase in 2026–27, and the DECA electronic transport document lands late 2026 — all forcing this exact cohort toward digital tools. Ride that wave (nearer-term than EU ViDA, which mostly bites ~2030).

8.3 Beachhead ICP

Who we onboard first

An international-running autónomo or micro-carrier (1–10 heavy trucks, ≥7.5t), based in Murcia / the Mediterranean corridor / Aragón, who holds a fuel card + Certificado Digital, currently uses a generalist gestor, and leaves foreign VAT & excise unclaimed. This driver has the highest recoverable pool and the lowest incumbent coverage.

Screen for these eligibility gates at recruitment

(1) VAT regime: carriers under módulos / régimen simplificado de IVA don't deduct input VAT invoice-by-invoice — their modelo 360 recovery is restricted; prioritise estimación directa operators. (2) ≥7.5t + Euro class for excise (Italy needs Euro 5+). (3) Fuel-card structure — confirm whose invoice the card issues (see fuel-card VAT wrinkle, §2.2), and that spend isn't already net-invoiced.

8.7 The make-or-break: onboarding & filing rights

Design this first

To file on a driver's behalf, no professional licence is required — Spain uses apoderamiento electrónico (AEAT registro de apoderamientos) or colaborador social status, plus the driver's Certificado Digital (FNMT, free) or Cl@ve. Most drivers already hold a certificate for their modelo 303. The apoderamiento + certificate flow is the single make-or-break UX — build it as a first-class, Spanish-language, hand-held onboarding step. Handling censo/bank data triggers AEAT security + GDPR duties.
