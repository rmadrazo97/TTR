Market Sizing

A €520B industry, ~600k operators, ~90% micro-fleets. Bottom-up, the recoverable tax pool is ~€10.8B/year — of which the underserved SME segment is ~€5.5B.

Fleet concentration — share of EU goods-vehicle stock:

  • Poland — 17.9% (~1.2M trucks; a broader base than the 6.2M medium/heavy figure above) and 32.7% of tonne-km — the clear leader.
  • Germany 14.0% · France 10.3% · Spain 9.7% — the next tier.
  • Then Italy, Netherlands, Romania, Lithuania (Lithuania highest per-capita).

The market is fragmented and international-heavy in exactly the countries with the richest recovery.

Assumes ~€60k gross diesel spend/truck/yr (~40,000 L). Cross-checks Eurowag's marketed "up to 20–22% of overseas costs."

Willingness to pay

Specialist reclaim agents charge ~5–12% of VAT recovered (often bundled with pre-financing); gestor / full-service arrangements effectively cost more. We model a blended 15% across the full bundle (VAT + excise + per-diem, self-serve support, pre-financing) — a bundle + self-serve wedge, not a price undercut, and flexible downward. At 15%:

€6,000 recovered × 15% ≈ €900 revenue per truck / year.

Per-diem income-tax relief adds driver-level upside but is a declining line (Mobility Package) — we exclude it from the core sizing.

3.5 Unit economics & revenue cadence

LTV / CAC
  • >3× if a carrier stays 3+ years (~€520–780 contribution/truck/yr) — churn contained.
  • Look-back (2–4× run-rate, §2.5) front-loads payback.
  • Illustrative — the pilot must return real cost-to-serve & CAC.
Cadence & cash lag
  • Gasóleo monthly · excise quarterly · dietas annual
  • VAT spikes 30-Sep → pays 4–8 mo later

Fee booked on cash → year-one revenue is thin; counter-seasonal excise + dietas smooth it. Plan runway.

Bottom line

~€1.6B success-fee TAM · ~€825M underserved SME slice — real even at conservative penetration. Validate the two soft inputs (truck count, fee %) early.
