Market Sizing

A €520B industry, ~600k operators, ~90% micro-fleets. Bottom-up, the recoverable tax pool is €10.8B/yr — of which the underserved SME segment is €5.5B.

Fleet concentration — share of EU goods-vehicle stock:

  • Poland — 17.9% (~1.2M trucks; a broader base than the 6.2M medium/heavy figure above) and 32.7% of tonne-km — the clear leader.

The market is fragmented and international-heavy in exactly the countries with the richest recovery.

Willingness to pay

Specialist reclaim agents charge ~5–12% of VAT recovered (often bundled with pre-financing); gestor / full-service arrangements effectively cost more. We model a blended 15% across the full bundle (VAT + excise + per-diem, self-serve support, pre-financing) — we win on the bundle and self-serve, not on price, and can go lower if needed. At 15%:

Revenue per truck

~€6,000 recovered × 15% ≈ €900 revenue / truck / yr.

Per-diem income-tax relief adds driver-level upside but is a declining line (Mobility Package) — we exclude it from the core sizing.

3.3 Two horizons — Spain first, the EU next

Same playbook, two timeframes. Spain is the concrete first market we can land now — roughly an 8% slice of the pool; the EU is the long-term ceiling once the Spain model proves out.

Spain · the first market — now → 12–18 mo

~€850M recoverable → ~€130M fee TAM (est) across ~145k international trucks (of Spain's ~360k total). We start with an 8-week pilot: 25–40 carriers → ~€55–110k fee. Spain Pilot →

€10.8B recoverable → €1.6B fee TAM across ~1.8M international trucks (SAM €5.5B). Full-EU rollout once the Spain playbook proves out.

3.5 Unit economics & cash timing

Payback · LTV vs CAC
  • Each truck earns us ~€520–780/yr. A carrier that stays 3+ years returns well over 3× what we spent to win it (as long as it doesn't leave).
  • Recovering past years (look-back, §2.5) makes year one 2–4× bigger — so we recoup that cost fast.
  • These are estimates — the pilot must prove the real costs.
Cash timing · lumpy & delayed
  • VAT (the biggest slice) is claimed by 30 Sep but paid 4–8 months later.
  • Excise refunds pay quarterly (Spain's gasóleo profesional monthly); per-diem (dietas) yearly — income is spread out.

We earn our fee only when the refund lands → year-one revenue is thin. Plan runway.

Bottom line

A €1.6B/yr fee TAM across the EU — €825M of it the underserved SME slice we target. It works even if we win only a little. Everything hinges on two estimates — how many international trucks, and our fee % — so validate them early.
