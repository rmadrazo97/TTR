Market Sizing

A €520B industry, ~600k operators, ~90% micro-fleets. Bottom-up, the recoverable tax pool is ~€10.8B/year — of which the underserved SME segment is ~€5.5B.

Fleet concentration: Poland leads at 17.9% of EU goods-vehicle stock (a broader base than the 6.2M medium/heavy figure above; ~1.2M trucks) and 32.7% of tonne-km, followed by Germany 14.0%, France 10.3%, Spain 9.7%, then Italy, Netherlands, Romania and Lithuania (highest per-capita). The market is fragmented and international-heavy in exactly the countries with the richest recovery.

Willingness to pay

Specialist reclaim agents charge ~5–12% of VAT recovered (often bundled with pre-financing); gestor / full-service arrangements effectively cost more. We model a blended 15% across the full bundle (VAT + excise + per-diem, self-serve support, pre-financing) — a bundle + self-serve wedge, not a price undercut, and flexible downward. At 15%:

€6,000 recovered × 15% ≈ €900 revenue per truck / year.

Per-diem income-tax relief adds driver-level upside but is a declining line (Mobility Package) — we exclude it from the core sizing.

Read the TAM honestly — recoverable ≠ unclaimed

A large share of the €10.8B is already recovered (card net-invoicing, agent-served, self-filed). The greenfield unclaimed prize is smaller; winning served money is share-steal (different pitch, higher CAC). We size the fee TAM at ~€1.6B but treat the near-term addressable-unclaimed pool as the honest target. The other load-bearing input — ~1.8M "international-active trucks" — needs a firmer Eurostat cross-check (int'l tonne-km share → implied truck count).

LTV/CAC: a multi-truck carrier that stays 3+ years at ~€400–800 contribution/truck/yr gives LTV/CAC well above 3× — if churn is contained. First-year look-back recovery (2–4× run-rate, see §2.5) front-loads payback. These are illustrative — the pilot must return real cost-to-serve and CAC.

Cadence & cash lag

Revenue is seasonal and lagged. VAT claims spike toward the 30-Sep deadline and pay 4–8 months later; excise runs quarterly; gasóleo monthly; dietas annual. Counter-seasonal products (excise, dietas) smooth the curve. Fee is booked on cash recovered → year-one revenue is thin; plan runway accordingly.

Bottom line

A ~€1.6B success-fee TAM, with a genuinely underserved €825M SME slice. The business is real even at conservative penetration. Sensitivity: ±30% on the two soft inputs (int'l-truck count, fee %) swings the fee TAM between ~€1.1B and ~€2.1B — validate both with real quotes early.
