Executive Summary

Europe's small road carriers overpay tax every year because reclaiming it is too complex, too manual, and priced for fleets. We turn a phone photo into a filed claim.

1.1 The problem

An EU truck crossing borders overpays — or simply never reclaims — three legally recoverable pools: (1) VAT on diesel, tolls, AdBlue and repairs bought abroad (Directive 2008/9/EC); (2) partial professional-diesel excise refunds in France, Belgium, Italy, Spain and others; and (3) per-diem / days-abroad income-tax relief for the driver. Combined, that is roughly €6,000 in fuel VAT + excise per international truck per year, plus driver income-tax relief on top. Most of it goes unclaimed because of invoice-format rules, multi-country portals, language, cost, and a hard 30 September deadline.

1.2 Who suffers most — and why they're our wedge

~90% of EU road-freight operators are micro-fleets and owner-operators. They are structurally underserved: fuel-card majors bundle recovery but only on their own card spend (a lock-in tax); reclaim specialists are consultative, success-fee, "email us your invoices" shops built for fleets, and the directive's own €400 quarterly / €50 annual minimums discourage the smallest operators. No player is simultaneously card-agnostic, self-serve, micro-priced, and bundling all three recoveries. That is the open lane.

What we build

A mobile-first, AI-native platform. Drivers forward a photo of a receipt (WhatsApp, email, or app); Claude-vision extraction + a per-country rules engine turn it into a validated, filing-ready claim. Billing is no-win-no-fee — a success fee on money actually recovered.

Why now

National e-invoicing mandates (Spain Verifactu, Italy, France 2026–27; EU ViDA following ~2030) make invoices machine-readable, collapsing the cost-to-serve the fragmented long tail. Margin pressure and a structural driver shortage make every €900/truck matter more. AI receipt extraction is finally good enough for money documents.

Self-serve PWA, dual-extractor pipeline, claim-readiness dashboard, success-fee billing. One flow end-to-end: foreign fuel VAT.

Mobile apps, fuel-card/TMS + tachograph ingest, multi-country rules engine, automated e-filing, white-label.

1.5 The recommendation

Go — with a disciplined Spanish beachhead

Start with Spanish international-running 1–10-truck carriers. Monetise the foreign VAT + excise they abandon today (~€6,000/truck/yr, manual and painful); use the already-familiar gasóleo profesional refund as a trust hook, not the revenue (it's already automated via fuel cards). Run an 8-week concierge pilot to validate the two load-bearing numbers — € recovered per truck and willingness to pay a success fee — and to prove the apoderamiento / digital-certificate onboarding, which is the real gate.
