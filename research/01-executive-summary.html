Executive Summary

Europe's small road carriers overpay tax every year because reclaiming it is too complex, too manual, and priced for fleets. We turn a phone photo into a filed claim.

1.1 The problem

An EU truck crossing borders overpays — or simply never reclaims — three legally recoverable pools:

  • Foreign VAT on diesel, tolls, AdBlue and repairs bought abroad (Directive 2008/9/EC).
  • Professional-diesel excise — partial refunds in France, Belgium, Italy, Spain and others.
  • Per-diem / days-abroad income-tax relief for the driver.

Combined, that is roughly €6,000 in fuel VAT + excise per international truck per year, plus driver income-tax relief on top. Most of it goes unclaimed because of invoice-format rules, multi-country portals, language, cost, and a hard 30 September deadline.

1.2 Who suffers most — and why they're our wedge

~90% of EU road-freight operators are micro-fleets and owner-operators — and structurally underserved:

  • Fuel-card majors bundle recovery, but only on their own card spend — a lock-in tax.
  • Reclaim specialists are consultative, success-fee, "email-us-your-invoices" shops built for fleets.
  • Minimum claim sizes. The VAT-refund directive won't accept a claim below €400 for a part-year filing (3–11 months) or €50 for the full year — so an operator with little foreign spend must wait to accumulate enough before it can reclaim anything, or skip it altogether.

No player is simultaneously card-agnostic, self-serve, micro-priced, and bundling all three recoveries. That is the open lane.

What we build

  • Mobile-first. Drivers forward a photo of a receipt (WhatsApp, email, or app).
  • Multimodal AI extraction (OCR + vision LLMs) plus a per-country rules engine turn it into a validated, filing-ready claim.
  • No-win-no-fee. A success fee on money actually recovered.

Why now

  • E-invoicing mandates (Spain Verifactu, Italy, France 2026–27; EU ViDA ~2030) make invoices machine-readable, collapsing the cost-to-serve the fragmented long tail.
  • Margin pressure + a structural driver shortage make every €900/truck matter more.
  • The enabling tech is finally mature and cheap. Commodity AI extraction (low-cost proprietary APIs + capable OSS models) drops cost-to-serve enough to make the micro-segment profitable at a ~15% fee.

1.5 The POC / Pilot

A focused Spanish pilot to prove the model before scaling — each step gates the next:

  1. Target the right carrierSpanish, internationally-running, 1–10 trucks.
  2. Monetise the money they abandon todayForeign VAT + excise left unclaimed — ~€6,000/truck/yr, today manual and painful.
  3. Lead with a trust hook, not the revenueThe familiar gasóleo profesional refund builds trust; it isn't the money (it's already automated via fuel cards).
  4. Run an 8-week concierge pilotHands-on, end-to-end — we file for the carrier.
  5. Validate the two make-or-break numbers€ recovered per truck, and willingness to pay a success fee.
  6. Clear the real gateApoderamiento / digital-certificate onboarding — the true bottleneck.
